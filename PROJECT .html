Introduction¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.

ScheduledDay tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment.

Neighborhood indicates the location of the hospital.

Scholarship indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia.

Be careful about the encoding of the last column: it says No if the patient showed up to their appointment, and Yes if they did not show up.

Dataset Description¶

In this project we have a set of data in csv form to analyze

Question(s) for Analysis¶

What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment? for example.

Question 1: Is there a interconnection between missing the appointment and the age of the patient?
Question 2: Is there a interconnection between missing the appointment and the Gender of the patient?
Question 3: Is there a interconnection between missing the appointment and not receiving an SMS message?
Question 4: Is there a interconnection between missing the appointment and the Neighbourhood?

#to check if age affects attending appointment
def attendance(df,col_name,attended,absent):
    plt.figure(figsize=[15,4])
    df[col_name][show_appointment].hist(alpha=0.5,bins=10,color='green',label='show_appointment')
    df[col_name][noshow_appointment].hist(alpha=0.5,bins=10,color='red',label='noshow_appointment')
    plt.legend();
    plt.title('Histogram of Age affecting Patients appointment')
    plt.xlabel('Age')
    plt.ylabel('Number of Patients');
attendance(df,'Age',show_appointment,noshow_appointment)

Observation:

There is no strong interconnection between age alone and missing the appointment, although kids and patients in their 60s, 70s and 80s are more likely to show to their appointments

Conclusion¶

After investigating the interconnection between some of the characteristics of the patients and whether they attended or missed their appointment, These are the results of these analyses:

Children and patients in their 60s, 70s, and 80s are likely to show to their appointments
Patients from Age 10 to mid-30s are more likely to miss their appointments
There is no interconnection between gender alone and missing the appointment.
Receiving an SMS did not increase the chance that the patient would show to their appointment.
Neighbourhood has a strong effect on whether a patient will attend the appointment or not.

Limitation¶

The Handcap and SMS_received columns were not clearly  described on the dataset page.
There was no clarification of how the data was collected. 
The dataset didn't clarify why patients comes to the doctor, which may give an additional clarification about why they miss their appointments.
